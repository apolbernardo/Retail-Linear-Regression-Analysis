# Retail Revenue Analysis & Forecasting – Linear Regression
Project Description: This project analyzes several years of retail transaction data to understand sales patterns, the impact of promotions, and to forecast future revenue.

Key Objectives:

Investigate the relationship between promotional activities and sales performance
Analyze how different factors (discounts, category, region) influence revenue
Build a simple Linear Regression model to forecast 2025 revenue
Provide actionable business recommendations to reduce over-reliance on discounts

What I Did:

Created Total_Revenue feature by multiplying Units Sold × Price
Performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on sales trends across years and categories
Conducted Linear Regression to understand the relationship between key variables and revenue
Visualized historical revenue trends and 2025 forecast
Identified promotion dependency and customer behavior shifts

Key Findings:

Sales in 2022–2023 showed a synthetic peak heavily driven by aggressive promotions.
In 2024, when promotions decreased, transaction volume dropped dramatically (>99%).
Customers shifted from essential goods (Groceries, Clothing) to discretionary items (Toys, Furniture) during low-promo periods.
Linear Regression revealed that certain variables (such as Length of Stay in related analysis or promotional intensity) significantly impact costs and revenue.

Technical Skills Demonstrated:

Data cleaning and feature engineering
Exploratory Data Analysis with Python (pandas, seaborn, matplotlib)
Linear Regression using scikit-learn
Data visualization and storytelling

Business Impact & Recommendations:
Recommended shifting from heavy blanket discounting to more strategic, data-driven promotions and building stronger organic demand through customer loyalty and product differentiation.
